# The Wandwright — visual spec

**REF-37**, 32x32, idle, transparent bg.
**Mission:** `generic_fn` — a function generic over `T` with trait bounds.

## Curriculum cue

A generic function works for any type that satisfies its bounds: `fn cast<T: Spell>(t: T)`. The bounds are the contract — anything that fits them can be passed in. The Wandwright turns out wand-blanks that will work for *any* element: the blank itself is plain and unbound, but its gold fittings are machined to a spec, and only a type that fits the spec can be set into it. The fittings are the trait bounds; the blank is the generic parameter `T`, not yet a concrete element.

## Visual brief

A lean craftsman in a working **Crypt** apron-tunic with **Oxblood** primary and **Wineflesh** lit folds, offset toward frame-right so the bench reads in the corner. In the raised left hand, a wand-blank: a **Stone grey** shaft with an **Aged paper** highlight at the tip and **Coalblack** outline — deliberately plain, no element bound yet. At the grip, the gold fittings: an **Antique brass** collar with an **Old gold** specular ferrule — the bounds the blank is machined to. The right hand is held open and empty.

In the lower frame-left corner, a small work-bench: a **Bronze** top over a **Bog umber** apron, **Coalblack** legs, with a single tool on it — an **Antique brass** head with an **Old gold** glint and a **Coalblack** haft.

Head: short **Stone grey** hair with **Aged paper** highlight, **Basalt** brow and eyes, skin in **Pink quartz** / **Dusty rose**. **Antique brass** belt band, **Pine** hem, **Bronze**/**Bog umber** boots.

## Palette compliance

Burgundy (tunic): `Y O W`. Skin: `P R`. Neutrals (hair, outline, wand blank): `X B S V`. Gold (wand fittings, bench top, tool, belt): `Z N G`. Bog umber `U` (bench apron, boots), Pine `F` (hem).

No magic violet — an unbound wand carries no element. No alarm scarlet, no specular white, no cobalt, no teal, no forest beyond the pine hem.

## Lighting

Top-left 45°. The raised blank catches **Aged paper** at the tip; the **Old gold** ferrule at the grip is the one bright fitting — the bounds, lit. The bench falls into **Bog umber** shadow in the corner so it stays subordinate to the figure.
