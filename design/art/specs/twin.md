# The Twin — visual spec

**REF-18**, 32×32, idle, transparent bg.
**Mission:** `tuple_destructure` — teaches `let (a, b) = pair`.

## Curriculum cue

A 2-tuple is two values bound in one shape; destructuring is the act of giving each half its own name. The Twin is *one figure whose left and right halves are different colors*, mirrored down a single-pixel central axis. The viewer's eye reads "two things, one outline" — that is the let-binding made flesh. The character has been a single person their whole life and their wardrobe is a daily reminder that they are also a pair.

## Visual brief

Single front-facing 32×32 humanoid, identical pose to the Smith / Bellringer. The tunic is **bisected vertically** down the centre column: the figure's left half (viewer's right) is **Oxblood** with **Wineflesh** mid and **Crypt** shadow; the figure's right half (viewer's left) is **Forest** with **Spring meadow** mid and **Pine** shadow. Skin and outline are unified across the centreline — same Pink quartz / Dusty rose ramp on both sides — so the read is "one body, two field-names."

Hair is split too: viewer's right is **Crypt** (warm), viewer's left is **Bronze** (cool-warm). One **Old gold** circlet across the brow ties the halves together — the parens `(...)` of the destructure.

Belt is **Stone grey** with a single **Antique brass** clasp at centre — the comma between the two pattern bindings.

Both arms hang at the sides; small detail: the left hand wears a single **Pink quartz** ring (`a`), the right hand a single **Hayfield** ring (`b`), echoing the named bindings on each side of the destructure.

## Palette compliance

Burgundy (left tunic, skin): `K Y O W R P`. Forest (right tunic, hair shadow): `J F E H Q`. Gold (circlet, belt clasp, ring): `Z N G`. Neutrals (outline, belt): `X B S`.

No magic violet, no alarm scarlet, no specular white, no cobalt blue, no teal.

## Lighting

Top-left 45° applies *per half*: each side gets its own internal highlight. The bisecting centre column reads sharp because the two ramps meet at maximum contrast.
