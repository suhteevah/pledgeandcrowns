# The Tallywright — visual spec

**REF-51**, 32x32, idle, transparent bg.
**Mission:** `iter_enumerate` — pair each item with its index via `.enumerate`.

## Curriculum cue

`.enumerate()` pairs every element with its running count, yielding `(i, item)` — it does not just hand you the items, it hands you each item *with its index*. The Tallywright embodies this: a row of items runs along a rail before him, and as each passes he brings a counter-stamp down and prints its number on it — 0, 1, 2 — so each carries both its place and itself. When the player writes `for (i, item) in iter.enumerate()`, they are doing the Tallywright's move — let each item pass, stamp its index onto it, hand back the pair.

## Visual brief

A figure standing, frame-right hand wielding a counter-stamp raised mid-stroke. **Crypt** clerk tunic with **Oxblood** primary and **Wineflesh** lit folds — the house burgundy, drawn slightly off-center so the rail of stamped items runs along the frame's lower edge. **Inkblood** seams the deep shadow.

In the raised frame-right hand, a **counter-stamp**: an **Old gold** stamp head with a **Brass leaf** specular pixel, on a **Bronze** shaft, **Coalblack**-outlined — the numbering tool. The **Pink quartz** / **Dusty rose** hand grips it.

Along the bottom of the frame, a low **Bronze rail** with **Antique brass** caps carrying a row of **Old gold** tags, each printed with a small **Coalblack**-digit mark (0, 1, 2) — the items, each now stamped with its index. A **Bog umber** shadow runs under the rail.

Head: short **Stone grey** hair with **Aged paper** highlights, **Basalt** brow and eye line, skin in **Pink quartz** / **Dusty rose**. **Antique brass** belt band, **Pine** hem, **Bronze**/**Bog umber** boots.

## Palette compliance

Burgundy (tunic): `K Y O W`. Skin (face, hand): `P R`. Neutrals (hair, outline, stamp shaft, rail, digits): `X B S V`. Gold (stamp head, numbered tags, rail caps, belt): `Z N G L`. Bog umber `U` (rail/boot shadow), Pine `F` (hem).

No magic violet, no alarm scarlet, no specular white, no cobalt, no teal, no forest beyond the pine hem. The **Old gold** stamp head and the **Old gold** numbered tags are the bright accents — the index made visible, printed on each item as it passes.

## Lighting

Top-left 45°. The **Brass leaf** spec on the stamp head is the brightest point, top-center where the tool is raised; the **Old gold** tags catch the light along the rail below, their **Coalblack** digits reading as the stamped indices.
