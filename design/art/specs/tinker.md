# The Tinker — visual spec

**REF-19**, 32×32, idle, transparent bg.
**Mission:** `while_loop` — teaches `while cond { ... }`.

## Curriculum cue

A `while` loop runs the body again and again *until the condition stops holding*. The Tinker is the village fix-it artisan whose whole craft is iteration — tighten, check, tighten, check, until the part is right. His sprite is busy with small repeating tools: a **belt of pouches**, a **mallet**, and a small **gear** clipped at the hip. Repetition is shown across the costume.

## Visual brief

Stocky build (shorter than the Smith, broader than the Bellringer). Wears a **leather hood** thrown back, exposing short **Bronze** hair. Goggles pushed up on his forehead — two adjacent **Stone grey** rectangles ringed in **Coalblack**, suggesting circular lenses. Forest-green vest (**Forest** primary, **Pine** shadow, **Spring meadow** highlight) over a **Bog umber** undershirt. Sleeves rolled to the elbow.

The **belt** is the focal point: alternating **Bronze** and **Bog umber** pouches (four total: brass-bronze-brass-bronze, one per pouch slot), reading as a row of repeated cells — the iterations of the loop. A small **Old gold** gear hangs at the right hip from a **Stone grey** chain (one or two pixels). Trousers are **Bog umber**; boots **Bronze** with **Coalblack** soles.

In his right hand, held loosely against the thigh, a small **mallet** — head **Basalt**, haft **Bronze**. Same rest-state pose as the Smith's hammer; the Tinker's tool is smaller because his work is finer.

Skin is the standard ramp. Eyes one Coalblack pixel each. Mouth a thin Wineflesh line, set in tinker-concentration.

## Palette compliance

Forest (vest): `J F E H Q`. Gold ramp (hair, belt-cells, gear, vest highlight, mallet haft): `U Z N G`. Neutrals (goggles, mallet head, outline, belt webbing): `X B S`. Skin: `P R W`. Burgundy (mouth, ear shadow): `W`.

No magic violet, no alarm scarlet, no specular white, no cobalt blue, no teal.

## Lighting

Top-left 45°. Highlights on the left edges of vest, hood, and mallet head. Goggle lenses pick up no specular (the bible reserves `#` for Borrow Checker reference glyphs and the title screen).
